## What this is

These are the 24 phase-by-phase checklists that drive your AI's understanding of how a build progresses on your job site. When the morning view tells you a phase is blocked, when the desktop app shows you a list of items to tap off, when the system decides a phase is ready to advance — these checklists are the source.

Each item is one of two kinds:

- **📷 Photo-required** — physical site work that the AI expects you to back with a photo when you tap it complete. Photos land in your Drive at `Site Logs/<Project>/Checklist Photos/<phase>/<item>/`. Photo evidence carries forward as legal/quality record.
- **Plain (no marker)** — administrative items: paperwork, scheduling, document filing. No photo expected; just a tick.

## How to redline

Open this document in Google Docs (or any editor where you can mark it up) and:

1. **Strike out items you don't actually do.** Anything you've never bothered with on real builds — delete.
2. **Add items I missed.** Something you always do that isn't here? Write it in.
3. **Flip the 📷 markers.** Items I marked photo-required but you don't really photograph — drop the marker. Items I missed that you'd want photographed — add it.
4. **Reorder if it doesn't match your flow.** I sequenced what felt natural; you know how you actually work.
5. **Comment in the margin** (Doc comments, or just inline notes) on anything you want me to see.

Send back when you're done. I'll incorporate every redline directly into the system. Estimated time: about an hour, total. You can do one phase per cup of coffee over a few mornings.

## Phase 2: Sitework

_Sitework phase closes when the lot is cleared, graded, and stabilized; underground utilities are stubbed and located; erosion controls are active and inspected; and the building pad is compacted, surveyed, and approved for foundation work to begin._

**39 items** · **📷 31 photo-required** (79%)

### Permits & Approvals On-Site (1/5 📷)

1. 📷 Building permit card posted on-site in weatherproof holder, visible from street
2. Septic / sewer permit approved and copy on-site (Baldwin County Health Department)
3. Land-disturbing activity permit confirmed if disturbed area exceeds 1 acre (ADEM NPDES requirement)
4. Driveway / access permit issued by Baldwin County Engineering if county road access is required
5. Wetlands determination letter on file; Army Corps 404 permit confirmed if any fill within jurisdictional wetlands

### Clearing & Demolition (5/5 📷)

1. 📷 Tree-save fence installed at drip line of all trees designated for preservation on site plan
2. 📷 Clearing limits staked and flagged to match approved site plan — no clearing beyond staked boundary
3. 📷 All trees, stumps, and organic debris removed from building footprint and within 10 feet of foundation perimeter
4. 📷 Demolished concrete, asphalt, or existing structure materials hauled off-site; no burial of organics or demolition debris on lot
5. 📷 Topsoil stripped from building footprint and stockpiled on-site per grading plan for reuse or disposal

### Erosion & Sediment Control (6/6 📷)

1. 📷 Silt fence installed along downslope perimeter of all disturbed areas before any grading begins
2. 📷 Construction entrance stabilized with 6-inch minimum compacted #57 stone pad, minimum 50 feet long, to prevent mud tracking onto road
3. 📷 Inlet protection installed on any storm drain inlets within 100 feet of disturbed area
4. 📷 Sediment basin or sediment trap installed if disturbed area exceeds threshold per ADEM SWPPP requirements
5. 📷 SWPPP binder on-site, signed by qualified person, with inspection log started — first inspection documented before grading commences
6. 📷 All erosion controls inspected by superintendent after first rain event and after any storm exceeding 0.5 inch; deficiencies corrected within 24 hours

### Grading & Drainage (4/5 📷)

1. 📷 Rough grade matches approved site plan finish floor elevation (FFE) within +/- 0.10 foot; FFE confirmed against flood zone BFE plus required freeboard
2. 📷 Positive drainage slope established away from building pad — minimum 6 inches of fall in first 10 feet on all sides per IRC R401.3
3. 📷 Swales cut to plan dimensions and flow direction confirmed by level; no ponding low spots within 20 feet of foundation
4. 📷 Fill material is clean select fill, free of organics, debris, and unsuitable soils; source and material type documented
5. Cut and fill quantities reconciled against grading plan; any deviation from plan approved in writing by engineer of record before proceeding

### Underground Utilities (6/6 📷)

1. 📷 Sewer lateral or septic system stub-out installed to within 5 feet of building footprint at correct invert elevation per engineered plan
2. 📷 Water service tap and meter set by Baldwin County Water Authority (or applicable utility); service line sleeved under any driveways or hardscape
3. 📷 Electrical service conduit stubbed to within 5 feet of meter location; conduit depth complies with NEC 300.5 burial depth requirements
4. 📷 Gas service line (if applicable) installed and pressure-tested per utility provider requirements before backfill
5. 📷 All underground utility locations marked with color-coded tracer wire or conduit; as-built sketch with depths documented and filed
6. 📷 All trench backfill compacted in maximum 8-inch lifts to 95% Standard Proctor per geotechnical report requirements; no water jetting used as compaction method

### Building Pad & Compaction (6/6 📷)

1. 📷 Geotechnical engineer or certified testing lab has proofrolled subgrade with loaded dump truck; no pumping, rutting, or soft spots observed
2. 📷 Compaction testing (nuclear gauge or sand cone) confirms minimum 95% Standard Proctor density at subgrade and at each 12-inch lift of fill — test reports signed and on file
3. 📷 Pad elevation surveyed by licensed surveyor and confirmed to match engineered finish floor elevation within +/- 0.05 foot
4. 📷 All tree stumps, roots larger than 1.5 inches diameter, and organic material removed from footprint to minimum 24 inches below finish subgrade
5. 📷 Vapor retarder or capillary break layer (if required by foundation engineer) placed and intact across full footprint before any form work begins
6. 📷 Superintendent and foundation sub have walked pad together and signed off that subgrade is acceptable to proceed to foundation

### Inspections & Sign-Off (3/6 📷)

1. 📷 Baldwin County Building Department setback / survey inspection passed (if required prior to foundation); inspection card signed
2. Compaction test reports reviewed and approved by project engineer of record in writing
3. 📷 SWPPP inspection log current with no unresolved deficiencies noted
4. 📷 Septic system installation inspected and approved by Baldwin County Health Department before any backfill over tank or field lines
5. Superintendent has completed site walk against this checklist; all open items resolved or written into a dated punch-log with assigned responsible party and due date
6. Client notified in writing that Sitework phase is closed and foundation phase is authorized to begin

## Phase 3: Foundation

_All foundation work — excavation through slab cure and anchor bolt verification — is complete, inspected, and approved before framing begins._

**37 items** · **📷 30 photo-required** (81%)

### Pre-Pour Verification (7/7 📷)

1. 📷 Building permit posted on-site and foundation inspection scheduled with Baldwin County Building Department
2. 📷 Foundation layout staked and verified against approved engineering drawings; setbacks confirmed with survey pins
3. 📷 Excavation depth matches engineered footing schedule; verified by superintendent with tape measure on all corners and load-bearing points
4. 📷 Soil bearing capacity confirmed by geo-tech or visual inspection per soils report on file; no soft spots, organic material, or standing water in bearing zones
5. 📷 Vapor barrier (minimum 6-mil poly, 10-mil preferred for $1M+ builds) installed, lapped minimum 12 inches at seams, and sealed at penetrations
6. 📷 All underground plumbing sleeves and conduit stubs set per MEP plan; locations confirmed with plumber before pour
7. 📷 Termite pre-treatment (soil treatment) completed by licensed pest control company; certificate issued and on file per Alabama Code

### Formwork & Reinforcement (7/7 📷)

1. 📷 Form dimensions match approved foundation plan; corners square to within 1/8 inch per 10 feet diagonal check
2. 📷 Rebar size, spacing, and lap lengths match structural engineering stamped drawings (e.g., #5 bars at 16" O.C. each way where specified)
3. 📷 Rebar clearance from earth maintained at minimum 3 inches on all sides; plastic chairs or equivalent used throughout — no wire-tied dirt contact
4. 📷 Monolithic thickened edges formed to engineered depth (typically 12–18 inches for Baldwin County coastal zone loads)
5. 📷 Post-tension cables or additional reinforcement installed per engineer's layout if post-tension slab specified; stressing pockets formed at correct locations
6. 📷 All penetrations (plumbing, electrical, HVAC) sleeved and blocked out per plan; no field-cut rebar around penetrations without engineer sign-off
7. 📷 Baldwin County foundation/footing inspection completed and inspection card signed before concrete is ordered

### Concrete Pour & Placement (5/7 📷)

1. Concrete mix design matches engineer's specification (minimum 3,000 PSI at 28 days; 4,000 PSI for coastal/high-humidity exposure preferred on $1M+ builds)
2. Concrete delivery tickets reviewed on arrival; mix, slump (max 4 inches without admixture approval), and load time verified
3. 📷 Concrete placed and consolidated without cold joints; vibrated at maximum 18-inch intervals around rebar and form edges
4. 📷 Slab finished to specified flatness tolerance: FF 25 / FL 20 minimum (F-number spec or straightedge max 3/16-inch gap under 10-foot straightedge)
5. 📷 Anchor bolt schedule installed per structural drawings — size, embedment depth, projection height, and spacing verified against framing bolt pattern before concrete sets
6. 📷 Slab elevation checked at minimum four corners and center with builder's level; maximum deviation from design elevation ±3/8 inch
7. 📷 Concrete test cylinders taken per ACI 301 (minimum one set of 3 cylinders per 50 CY or per day's pour); ticket numbers logged in project file

### Cure & Protection (4/5 📷)

1. 📷 Curing compound applied immediately after final finish, or wet-cure method (burlap + poly) maintained for minimum 7 days per ACI 308
2. 📷 Slab protected from rain damage, UV, and foot traffic for minimum 24 hours; form removal not before 24 hours or per engineer's direction
3. 28-day break strength confirmed from cylinder tests at ≥ specified PSI; test results on file before framing loads are applied
4. 📷 Post-tension cables stressed (if applicable) at 75% design strength per engineer's schedule; stressing log signed by contractor and on file
5. 📷 Slab inspected for cracks after cure: hairline shrinkage cracks noted and photographed; any crack wider than 1/16 inch or with vertical offset escalated to structural engineer before framing

### Anchor Bolts & Sill Plate Prep (5/5 📷)

1. 📷 All anchor bolt locations verified against approved framing plan; bolts plumb (within 1/8 inch), correct projection above slab for specified sill plate thickness plus nut and washer
2. 📷 Anchor bolt thread condition inspected; no damaged threads, cement fouling, or misalignment that would prevent full nut engagement
3. 📷 Sill plate pressure-treated species and grade confirmed per plans (minimum PT 0.40 ACQ for ground contact/slab interface in Baldwin County coastal environment)
4. 📷 Termite shield (metal or approved barrier) specified on drawings installed under sill plate where required by plan or code
5. 📷 Slab surface at sill plate bearing zone ground or shimmed flat to within 1/8 inch over any 3-foot run; no rocking or gap that would cause point loading on framing

### Inspections & Sign-Off (2/6 📷)

1. 📷 Baldwin County final foundation inspection passed; inspection card signed and photographed
2. 📷 Superintendent final walk of slab: all penetration sleeves capped, no debris embedded in surface, no rebar tips exposed
3. Slab elevation survey completed and elevations recorded in project file; superintendent and PM have both reviewed and approved
4. Foundation phase photos uploaded to project Drive folder (pre-pour reinforcement, anchor bolts, finish surface, inspection card)
5. Any field deviations from engineered drawings documented, RFI issued, and engineer written response on file before framing begins
6. Foundation phase marked complete in project schedule; framing crew start date confirmed with foreman

5. Any HOA or coastal setback approval documents received and on file

3. 📷 Slab flatness tolerance verified — maximum 3/16" in 10 ft for living areas per spec

5. 📷 Foundation dimensions field-verified against architectural plans: overall footprint, bearing wall centerlines, garage slab drop, step-downs at covered porches
6. 📷 Slab curing complete — minimum 7 days (or engineer's specified cure time) before framing load applied

### Site Conditions (6/6 📷)

1. 📷 Erosion controls (silt fence, inlet protection) inspected and intact before framing activity increases disturbed area

4. 📷 Dumpster and debris staging area established and not blocking material delivery or crane path
5. 📷 Temporary power (100A min. construction meter) energized and GFCI-protected outlets functional
6. 📷 Portable restroom on-site and positioned per OSHA 29 CFR 1926.51 proximity requirements

### Materials On-Site & Staged (7/7 📷)

1. 📷 Lumber package delivered, quantity verified against framing takeoff, and inspected for species/grade matching structural engineer's spec (typically #2 SPF or better)
2. 📷 Lumber stored off ground on bearers, covered with breathable tarps, stacked by member type for efficient crane or manual set
3. 📷 Engineered lumber (LVL, I-joists, PSL) confirmed on delivery ticket against structural engineer's beam and header schedule; no substitutions without EOR sign-off
4. 📷 Window and exterior door rough opening headers and support hardware on-site and cross-referenced to window/door schedule on approved plans
5. 📷 Structural hardware package (holdowns, straps, joist hangers, post caps, anchor bolt plates) on-site and verified against structural engineer's hardware schedule
6. 📷 Treated sill plate lumber (min. 0.40 CCA or approved equivalent per IRC R317) on-site and confirmed pressure-treated stamp visible
7. 📷 Sill seal gasket material on-site for full perimeter installation under all bottom plates bearing on concrete

### Crew & Schedule (0/6 📷)

1. Framing crew lead confirmed start date in writing; crew size and composition verified as sufficient for schedule (crew size documented in superintendent's log)
2. Framing foreman has reviewed approved structural plans, engineer's hardware schedule, and anchor bolt layout before mobilization
3. Crane or telehandler reserved and delivery/pickup dates confirmed if required by structural plan (two-story, heavy timber, long LVL spans)
4. Framing sub's certificate of insurance (GL + WC) current and on file; expiration dates checked against project duration
5. Framing schedule milestone dates communicated to MEP subs (rough-in follows frame) and confirmed they are still holding their reserved slots
6. Superintendent site walk scheduled with framing foreman the morning of Day 1 to review plan set, critical dims, and quality expectations before work begins

### Pre-Framing Superintendent Sign-Off (2/4 📷)

1. 📷 Superintendent has personally walked the slab and verified no condition exists that would prevent compliant framing (cracked concrete, mis-set anchors, out-of-square footprint) — deficiencies resolved or EOR-approved before proceeding
2. Project manager notified in writing (text or email) that all Pre-framing items are closed and framing is authorized to begin
3. 📷 Baseline framing photos taken and uploaded to project Drive folder (all four elevations of slab, anchor bolt layout, staged materials)
4. Any open RFIs or plan discrepancies identified during Pre-framing review submitted to architect and resolved before Day 1 of framing

2. 📷 All exterior door rough openings sized per door schedule; king studs, trimmers, and headers count confirmed

4. 📷 All rough openings plumb, square, and level (diagonal tolerance ≤⅛"); sill and jack stud condition verified

2. 📷 House wrap or WRB (weather-resistant barrier) installed continuously over sheathing, lapped shingle-fashion (top over bottom), taped at seams per manufacturer spec
3. 📷 All window and door rough openings flashed with self-adhering flashing tape per manufacturer's installation sequence before windows are set
4. 📷 WRB penetrations (hose bibs, electrical, HVAC) sealed and flashed

2. 📷 HVAC duct chase locations, air-handler platforms, and attic access framed per mechanical drawings
3. 📷 Electrical panel location framed and backing installed; sub-panel locations confirmed
4. 📷 Fire-blocking installed at all required locations per IRC R302.11 — top and bottom plates at floor levels, around penetrations, at soffits

### Inspections (5/5 📷)

1. 📷 Baldwin County framing inspection scheduled and passed — inspection card signed and posted on-site
2. 📷 Baldwin County wind mitigation inspection (if required for coastal insurance compliance) completed and documentation secured
3. 📷 Structural engineer or third-party inspector walk completed if required by project specs or lender; written sign-off on file
4. 📷 Superintendent final framing punch walk completed; all open items resolved before MEP subs mobilize
5. 📷 Superintendent photo documentation of framing, anchor bolts, connector hardware, and sheathing nailing uploaded to project Drive folder

### Quality & Sign-Off (4/5 📷)

2. 📷 All doubled joists, beams, and columns glued and nailed per structural requirements; no dry stacking of engineered lumber

### Structural & Connection Quality (6/6 📷)

2. 📷 Roof-to-wall connections confirmed: hurricane straps or clips (H2.5A or per wind-load schedule) installed at every rafter/truss bearing point per Baldwin County wind zone requirements
3. 📷 Floor system: rim joist blocking, joist hangers fully nailed (no missing or partially driven fasteners), blocking at bearing points per schedule
4. 📷 All hold-down hardware (HDU/PHD anchors) installed, snugged, and rod couplers accessible for MEP routing coordination
5. 📷 Stair framing: rise/run uniform within 3/8-inch total variation, stringers bearing minimum 3-1/2 inches on framing, blocking complete
6. 📷 Cantilevered elements and balcony framing confirmed as-built per engineer's detail — no unapproved field changes

### Sheathing & Weather Barrier (5/5 📷)

1. 📷 All wall sheathing (typically 7/16" or 15/32" OSB/plywood) installed with correct nail pattern per shear schedule — 6" field, 3" panel edges where required
2. 📷 Roof sheathing fully applied, H-clips in place at unsupported edges, no gaps exceeding 1/8" at panel joints
3. 📷 House wrap (Tyvek or equal) fully installed, lapped correctly (horizontal seams lapped over lower course), taped at all seams and penetrations
4. 📷 Flashing installed at all window and door rough openings (sill pan flashing, head flashing) prior to window installation
